The Goose Index now has a blog. This is the first entry, and mostly a note on
what belongs here.

## What this is

Notes from building and running the site. The pages themselves try to say as
little as possible — a chart, its evidence, and a heading that asks a question
instead of asserting an answer. That discipline leaves no room to explain *why*
a chart is a dial and not a bar, or what the nightly sync actually does at
2 a.m., or what broke last week and how it got fixed. Those notes now have a
place to live.

## What this is not

- Not setlist news. [elgoose.net](https://elgoose.net) is the record;
  this site caches it nightly and credits it on every page.
- Not a second statistics page. A number in prose goes stale the night the
  sync runs; anything worth computing is computed at render time on
  [stats](/stats), and the blog will point there rather than repeat it.
- Not a feed you need to check. There is [RSS](/blog/feed.xml), with full
  posts in it.

## Where it starts

As this is written (July 2026), the record opens at [[show:2014-09-27]], and
the sync extends it every night. The blog starts here, thirteen years in —
which feels about right for a site whose whole premise is writing things down
after they happen.

If you want to know how these pages get from a markdown file to your feed
reader, the next post — [How this blog works](/blog/how-this-blog-works) — is
the engine, documented.
